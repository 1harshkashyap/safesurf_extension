Fake Login Pages — #1 Credential Threat
Attackers clone login pages of banks, email, and social networks pixel-by-pixel. SafeSurf v4 detects these by simultaneously analysing domain legitimacy, password fields, brand imagery, and login language — flagging what a URL check alone misses.
🧬
Adaptive Trust — SafeSurf Learns Your Behaviour
Every site you visit safely is remembered. After 2+ safe visits, SafeSurf increases trust confidence for that domain. After 10+ visits, it provides a significant trust boost. This is how the system becomes personalised to your browsing patterns over time.
⚗️
Attack Simulation — See the Actual Threat
SafeSurf's Attack Simulation panel shows exactly what would happen if a detected risk were exploited — what data could be stolen, what the consequences are, and how difficult the attack is. This makes abstract risks concrete and understandable.
🧠
Phishing Intent — Beyond Keywords
SafeSurf analyses WHERE pressure language appears. Urgency words in an H1 heading carry 4× more weight than body text. Action buttons carry 3× weight. This prevents false positives on legitimate sites that happen to use words like "urgent" in a news article.
🔗
URL Anatomy — Reading the Real Domain
In "paypal.secure-verify.com" the actual domain is "secure-verify.com" — not PayPal. The real domain is always the part immediately before the TLD (.com/.net/etc.). Attackers exploit the fact that users scan URLs from left to right and stop at the first familiar word.
🔬
Smart False Positive Filtering
SafeSurf v4 distinguishes between dangerous forms and benign ones. Search forms, GET-method forms, and forms on trusted domains are automatically classified as low-risk, even if they lack CSRF tokens. Only POST forms collecting sensitive data on unverified domains trigger warnings.
